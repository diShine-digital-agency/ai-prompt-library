# Product Photography Prompt

Generate studio-quality product photography prompts for e-commerce, advertising, and brand marketing. This template applies professional photography terminology—lighting setups, camera angles, and composition rules—to produce commercial-grade visuals.

## When to Use

- Creating product images for e-commerce listings or catalogs
- Generating lifestyle shots for social media marketing
- Producing hero images for landing pages or advertisements
- Visualizing product concepts before physical prototyping
- Building a consistent brand aesthetic across a product line

**When NOT to use:**

- When you need exact photorealistic replicas of existing products
- For technical product diagrams or exploded-view illustrations
- When precise color matching to Pantone specifications is required

## The Technique

Effective product photography prompts combine five pillars: **subject clarity**, **lighting direction**, **background context**, **camera perspective**, and **emotional mood**. Specify the product with material details (matte, glossy, metallic), describe the light source (softbox, rim light, golden hour), define the environment (seamless white, lifestyle scene), and set the camera angle (hero shot, flat lay, 45-degree). Adding a mood keyword steers the overall feel of the generated image.

### Lighting Glossary

- **Key light**: Primary light source defining the product shape
- **Rim light**: Edge lighting that separates subject from background
- **Softbox diffusion**: Even, shadow-free commercial lighting
- **Golden hour**: Warm, directional natural light for lifestyle shots
- **Beauty dish**: Focused, contrasty light ideal for glossy or reflective surfaces
- **Strip light**: Narrow light source creating elongated highlights on cylindrical products

### Background Strategies

- **Seamless paper**: Clean, infinite-look studio backdrop in any color
- **Lifestyle flat lay**: Curated arrangement with complementary props
- **Contextual environment**: Product shown in its natural usage setting
- **Gradient sweep**: Smooth tonal transition for modern, editorial aesthetics
- **Textured surface**: Marble, wood, or fabric adding tactile context to the shot

## Template

```
Professional product photography of {{product_name}}, a {{product_type}}.
Background: {{background_style}}.
Lighting: {{lighting}}, with subtle fill light to preserve shadow detail.
Camera angle: {{angle}}, shot with a 85mm prime lens at f/2.8 for shallow depth of field.
Mood: {{mood}}.
High resolution, 8K, photorealistic, commercial quality, color-accurate rendering.
No watermarks, no text overlays, no artifacts.
```

## Tips

1. **Specify material finishes** — "brushed aluminum," "matte ceramic," or "glossy acrylic" dramatically change how light interacts with the product surface.
2. **Name the lens** — Mentioning focal length (e.g., 85mm, 50mm macro) guides the AI toward realistic optical characteristics like bokeh and perspective compression.
3. **Add negative prompts** — Explicitly exclude unwanted elements: "no logos, no text, no human hands, no distortion."
4. **Use color temperature** — Terms like "cool daylight 5600K" or "warm tungsten 3200K" set the white balance and overall tone.
5. **Layer lighting sources** — Combining "key light from left, rim light from behind, bounce fill from below" produces dimensional, professional results.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Vague product descriptions** — "A bottle" produces generic results; "a tall cylindrical amber glass bottle with a brushed gold pump cap" is far more effective.
2. **Ignoring background interaction** — The background should complement the product; a busy environment steals focus from the subject.
3. **Over-prompting with conflicting styles** — Requesting "studio lighting" and "outdoor golden hour" simultaneously confuses the model.
4. **Skipping aspect ratio** — Not specifying the output ratio leads to awkward crops; use 1:1 for social, 4:5 for e-commerce, 16:9 for banners.
5. **Forgetting reflections and shadows** — Products on reflective surfaces need explicit mention: "subtle reflection on glossy surface, soft contact shadow."
