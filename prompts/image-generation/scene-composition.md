# Cinematic Scene Composition

Craft cinematic scene prompts that leverage film photography techniques—camera angles, lens choices, lighting setups, and color grading—to produce dramatic, story-driven visuals. This template brings the language of cinematography into AI image generation for maximum visual impact.

## When to Use

- Creating hero images with cinematic drama for marketing campaigns
- Generating concept art for film, TV, or game pre-production
- Producing editorial visuals with narrative depth for articles or covers
- Building mood boards for creative direction and visual storytelling
- Designing book covers, album art, or poster concepts with dramatic composition

**When NOT to use:**

- When you need storyboard sequences with consistent characters across frames
- For technical cinematography diagrams or camera setup references
- When frame-accurate recreation of existing film stills is required

## The Technique

Cinematic composition prompts borrow the language of filmmaking to guide AI toward visually powerful results. The five pillars are: **scene description** (setting, subjects, action), **genre framing** (noir, sci-fi, western), **camera angle** (Dutch angle, low angle, bird's eye), **lighting setup** (chiaroscuro, motivated practical lights, volumetric fog), and **color palette** (teal and orange, desaturated, monochromatic). The aspect ratio should match cinematic standards—2.39:1 for anamorphic widescreen or 16:9 for standard widescreen.

### Camera Angle Reference

- **Eye level**: Neutral, documentary feel
- **Low angle**: Subject appears powerful, imposing, heroic
- **High angle**: Subject appears vulnerable, small, overwhelmed
- **Dutch angle**: Tilted frame creates tension, unease, disorientation
- **Bird's eye**: God-like perspective, reveals spatial relationships
- **Over-the-shoulder**: Creates intimacy, suggests conversation or confrontation

## Tips

1. **Use motivated lighting** — "Light from the fireplace casting warm shadows" is more cinematic than "warm lighting" because it has a narrative source.
2. **Specify the lens type** — "Anamorphic 40mm" produces the horizontal lens flares and oval bokeh associated with blockbuster cinematography.
3. **Add atmospheric effects** — Fog, rain, dust particles, or volumetric god rays add depth layers and create the sense of a real physical environment.
4. **Reference genre conventions** — Genre keywords like "noir," "western," or "cyberpunk" activate strong visual associations in the model's training data.
5. **Use the teal-orange palette** — This complementary color scheme is the dominant Hollywood color grade; specifying it produces immediately cinematic-looking results.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Overwriting the scene** — Describing every object in the frame reduces cinematic impact; focus on 2–3 key visual elements and let the AI fill the environment.
2. **Ignoring aspect ratio** — Default square or 4:3 ratios feel non-cinematic; always use 16:9 or 2.39:1 for widescreen drama.
3. **Flat lighting descriptions** — "Well lit" produces corporate photography; cinematic scenes need contrast, shadows, and directional light sources.
4. **No depth layers** — Great compositions have foreground, midground, and background elements; mention all three for dimensional images.
5. **Contradicting genre with palette** — A horror scene with bright pastels or a romance with desaturated grays breaks genre expectations and produces incoherent results.
