# Professional Portrait & Headshot

Create polished portrait and headshot prompts for professional profiles, editorial use, and personal branding. This template leverages portrait photography fundamentals—focal length, lighting ratios, and expression direction—to produce flattering, natural-looking results.

## When to Use

- Generating professional headshots for LinkedIn or company directories
- Creating author photos for blogs, books, or speaker pages
- Producing editorial-style portraits for articles or interviews
- Designing avatar concepts for personal or brand identity
- Visualizing character references for creative projects

**When NOT to use:**

- When you need an exact likeness of a real person (AI cannot replicate real individuals reliably)
- For passport or ID photos with strict regulatory requirements
- When photographic authenticity with verifiable metadata is required

## The Technique

Portrait prompts succeed when they define four layers: **subject description** (age range, expression, attire), **photographic style** (editorial, corporate, casual), **lighting setup** (Rembrandt, butterfly, split), and **background treatment** (bokeh, gradient, environmental). Adding a mood keyword controls whether the result feels approachable, authoritative, or creative.

### Portrait Lighting Patterns

- **Rembrandt lighting**: Dramatic triangle of light on the shadow-side cheek
- **Butterfly lighting**: Centered overhead light creating a shadow under the nose
- **Loop lighting**: Slightly off-center key light, most universally flattering
- **Split lighting**: Half the face lit, half in shadow for dramatic effect
- **Broad lighting**: Illuminates the side of the face facing the camera, widening appearance
- **Short lighting**: Illuminates the side facing away, slimming and adding depth

### Background Selection Guide

- **Gradient studio**: Smooth tonal transition, timeless corporate feel
- **Environmental**: Office, lab, or workshop setting for contextual storytelling
- **Textured wall**: Concrete, brick, or painted surfaces for editorial edge
- **Pure white/black**: High contrast isolation for maximum subject focus

## Tips

1. **Describe expression, not emotion** — "Slight closed-lip smile with relaxed brow" is more actionable than "happy" for the model.
2. **Specify catchlights** — Mentioning "catchlights in the eyes" adds life and realism to AI-generated portraits.
3. **Control depth of field** — "85mm f/1.4 with bokeh" produces the classic portrait separation between subject and background.
4. **Preserve skin texture** — Add "natural skin texture, visible pores, no airbrushing" to avoid the plastic look common in AI portraits.
5. **Use aspect ratio intentionally** — 4:5 for Instagram, 1:1 for avatars, 2:3 for editorial layouts.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Overly specific facial features** — Listing every facial detail often produces uncanny results; keep descriptions natural and broad.
2. **Ignoring hand placement** — If hands are in frame, AI models frequently distort them; crop above the chest or explicitly specify hand position.
3. **Conflicting lighting and mood** — Requesting "bright butterfly lighting" with a "dark moody" feel creates contradictions.
4. **No background separation** — Without specifying bokeh or background blur, the subject merges visually with the environment.
5. **Forgetting wardrobe details** — Clothing texture and color significantly affect the overall composition; always describe attire.
