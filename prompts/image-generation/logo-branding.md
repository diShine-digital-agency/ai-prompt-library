# Logo & Brand Identity

Generate logo concepts and brand identity visuals for businesses, products, and personal brands. This template applies professional brand design principles—simplicity, scalability, and memorability—to produce versatile logo concepts suitable for further refinement in vector tools.

## When to Use

- Exploring logo concepts during early brand development
- Generating mood boards and visual direction for brand identity projects
- Creating icon or logomark ideas for apps and digital products
- Producing brand concept presentations for client pitch decks
- Designing monogram or wordmark variations for personal brands

**When NOT to use:**

- When you need a final production-ready vector logo (AI generates raster images)
- For exact typography-based wordmarks requiring specific font licensing
- When precise brand guideline specifications (spacing, clear zones) are needed
- For trademark-ready designs requiring legal originality verification

## The Technique

Effective logo prompts focus on the core principles of logo design: **simplicity** (must work at 16×16 favicon size), **memorability** (one strong visual idea), **versatility** (works in monochrome and color), and **relevance** (connects to the industry or brand values). Specify the logo type (icon, wordmark, combination mark, emblem), the visual metaphor or symbolism, and the design style (geometric, organic, vintage, modern minimal). Always request the logo on a clean background for maximum clarity.

### Logo Types

- **Logomark/Symbol**: Pure icon or symbol (Apple, Nike swoosh)
- **Wordmark**: Stylized text of the brand name (Google, Coca-Cola)
- **Combination mark**: Icon plus text (Adidas, Burger King)
- **Emblem**: Text enclosed within a symbol (Starbucks, Harley-Davidson)
- **Monogram**: Initials stylized as a mark (HBO, IBM)
- **Abstract mark**: Non-representational geometric shape conveying brand essence
- **Mascot**: Character-based logo adding personality and approachability

### Design Style Directions

- **Geometric minimalism**: Clean lines, mathematical precision, modern tech feel
- **Hand-crafted vintage**: Textured, badge-style, artisanal warmth
- **Organic/fluid**: Flowing curves, natural forms, approachable softness

## Template

```
Logo design for {{brand_name}}, a {{industry}} brand.
Style: {{style}}, conveying professionalism and modernity.
Colors: {{colors}}.
Symbolism: {{symbolism}}, creating an immediate visual association with the brand.
Usage: designed to work as {{usage}}.
Clean isolated design on a plain white background, centered composition.
Scalable from favicon to billboard, works in single color and full color.
Minimal detail, strong geometric structure, balanced proportions.
No photorealistic elements, no gradients unless specified, no background textures.
```

## Tips

1. **Request isolated backgrounds** — Always specify "on a plain white background" to get clean, easily extractable logo concepts.
2. **Specify a single visual metaphor** — The best logos communicate one idea; "a mountain that also looks like an arrow" is a clear brief for the model.
3. **Test at small sizes** — Add "must be legible at 32×32 pixels" to push the AI toward simpler, more scalable designs.
4. **Include monochrome instruction** — Adding "works in single color" encourages simpler designs that survive black-and-white reproduction.
5. **Iterate on variations** — Generate 4–5 concepts with different symbolism, then refine the strongest one with follow-up prompts.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too much detail** — Intricate illustrations fail at small sizes; logos must be simple enough to recognize instantly at any scale.
2. **Relying on AI for typography** — AI-generated text in logos is almost always distorted; plan to set the wordmark in a proper font editor.
3. **Requesting final production files** — AI generates raster concepts; always plan to recreate the chosen concept as a vector in Illustrator or Figma.
4. **Skipping the symbolism direction** — Without a clear visual metaphor, the model produces generic or irrelevant imagery for the brand.
5. **Ignoring versatility** — A logo that only works on dark backgrounds or at large sizes is not practical; specify multiple use contexts in the prompt.
