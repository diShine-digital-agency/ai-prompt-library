# LinkedIn content strategy

Templates and frameworks for LinkedIn content that builds authority, drives engagement, and generates business opportunities. These patterns are based on what performs well on the platform algorithmically and what resonates with professional audiences.

## When to use

- Building thought leadership and personal brand
- Content marketing for B2B products or services
- Career positioning and professional visibility
- Lead generation through content
- Company page content strategy

## The technique

### Algorithm-optimized post formats

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards certain patterns. These formats consistently outperform generic posts.

**The hook-story-insight format:**

```
Write a LinkedIn post using this structure:

HOOK (first 2 lines, visible before "see more"):
Start with a surprising statement, counterintuitive insight, or
relatable frustration. This must make the reader tap "see more."
Do NOT start with "I'm excited to announce" or "Thrilled to share."

Examples of strong hooks:
- "I fired our best-performing salesperson. Here's why it was the right call."
- "The worst advice I got as a founder: 'move fast and break things.'"
- "We spent $200K on a marketing campaign that generated zero leads. And I'd do it again."

STORY (4-8 lines):
Share a specific experience. Include details that make it real:
names (if appropriate), numbers, timeline. Show vulnerability or
an unexpected twist. Write in short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each).

INSIGHT (2-3 lines):
What did you learn? Make it applicable to the reader's situation.
Frame as a takeaway, not a lecture.

CTA (1 line):
Ask a question that invites comments. "What's your experience with this?"
or "Am I wrong about this?" work well.

FORMATTING RULES:
- One sentence per line for readability
- Use line breaks between paragraphs (LinkedIn compresses without them)
- No hashtags in the body -- put 3-5 at the end
- No emojis in every line (one or two max, if natural)
- Ideal length: 800-1200 characters
- Write as a human, not as a brand

Topic: {{topic}}
Audience: {{target_audience}}
Key message: {{takeaway}}
Personal angle: {{your_relevant_experience}}
```

### Thought leadership templates

**The contrarian take:**

```
Write a LinkedIn post that challenges conventional wisdom.

Conventional belief: "{{common_belief}}"
Your counter-argument: "{{your_position}}"
Evidence: {{supporting_evidence}}

Structure:
Line 1: State the popular opinion everyone agrees with
Line 2: "I disagree." or "This is wrong." (pattern interrupt)
Lines 3-8: Explain why with specific evidence
Lines 9-10: Acknowledge when the conventional wisdom IS true
Final line: Question to the audience

Tone: confident but not arrogant. Acknowledge nuance.
```

**The framework post:**

```
Share a practical framework on {{topic}}.

Structure:
Line 1: Problem statement the reader recognizes
Line 2: "Here's the framework I use:" (promise of value)
Lines 3-12: The framework in numbered steps (3-5 steps)
  - Each step: action + brief explanation + example
Lines 13-14: When NOT to use this framework (shows nuance)
Final line: "Save this for later" or ask which step resonates most

Topic: {{topic}}
Your framework: {{framework_steps}}
```

**The data-driven insight:**

```
Write a LinkedIn post centered on a surprising data point.

Data point: {{statistic_or_finding}}
Source: {{source}}
Your interpretation: {{what_this_means}}

Structure:
Line 1: The number or data point (makes people stop scrolling)
Line 2: Why this matters in plain language
Lines 3-6: Your analysis -- what most people miss about this data
Lines 7-8: What to do about it (practical takeaway)
Final line: Question about their experience with this

Include the source citation naturally in the text.
```

### Carousel post outline generator

```
Create a LinkedIn carousel post on {{topic}}.

SLIDE 1 — COVER:
- Bold headline (5-7 words)
- Subtitle that promises specific value
- Design: clean, one color accent, no stock photos

SLIDES 2-8 — CONTENT:
For each slide:
- One key point (headline, 5-8 words)
- 2-3 bullet points or a brief explanation
- Keep text minimal -- carousels are visual
- Each slide should make sense on its own (people skip around)

SLIDE 9 — SUMMARY:
- Recap all key points as a checklist
- "Save this post" prompt

SLIDE 10 — CTA:
- What to do next (follow, comment, visit link)
- Your name and what you do (branding)

POST CAPTION:
- Hook that makes people swipe
- Brief context
- 3-5 hashtags

Topic: {{topic}}
Audience: {{audience}}
Number of slides: {{8-12}}
Visual style: {{minimal / bold / infographic}}
```

### Comment strategy prompts

```
I want to build visibility through strategic commenting on LinkedIn.

My expertise: {{expertise_areas}}
Target audience: {{who_I_want_to_reach}}
Goal: {{awareness / lead_gen / networking / hiring}}

Generate 10 comment templates for different scenarios:

1. When an industry leader shares a hot take:
   [thoughtful addition, not just agreement]

2. When someone shares a failure or lesson learned:
   [relate with a similar experience, add insight]

3. When someone asks for recommendations:
   [provide specific, helpful recommendation with reasoning]

4. When a post contains data you can add context to:
   [additional data point or alternative interpretation]

5. When someone in your target audience posts for the first time:
   [encouraging, specific compliment + question]

RULES:
- Never comment just "Great post!" -- add substance
- Aim for 2-4 sentences (long enough to add value, short enough to be read)
- Ask a follow-up question when possible (drives replies)
- Reference something specific from their post (shows you read it)
- No self-promotion in comments unless directly relevant
```

### Personal branding narrative templates

```
Help me craft my LinkedIn personal brand narrative.

Background:
- Current role: {{role}}
- Industry: {{industry}}
- Key expertise: {{expertise}}
- Career path: {{brief_career_summary}}
- What I want to be known for: {{desired_positioning}}

Generate:

1. LINKEDIN HEADLINE (under 120 characters):
   Format: {{role}} | {{value_you_provide}} | {{proof_point}}
   Example: "VP Engineering | Helping teams ship 2x faster with async-first culture | Ex-Stripe, Ex-Shopify"

2. ABOUT SECTION (2000 chars max):
   Paragraph 1: who you help and what you help them achieve
   Paragraph 2: your credibility (experience, results, perspective)
   Paragraph 3: what you post about and why to follow you
   Last line: how to reach you

3. CONTENT PILLARS (3-4 themes you'll post about):
   Each pillar: topic + why you're credible on it + example post idea

4. WEEKLY POSTING RHYTHM:
   Suggested schedule based on your pillars
   Mix of: original posts, carousel shares, comments, reposts with takes
```

## Tips

1. **The first two lines are everything** -- on mobile, only the first 2-3 lines are visible. If the hook does not compel a tap on "see more," nothing else matters.

2. **Post consistently, not constantly** -- 3-4 posts per week outperforms daily posting. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards quality engagement per post, not volume.

3. **Reply to every comment within the first hour** -- comments in the first 60 minutes dramatically boost distribution. Reply thoughtfully, not with "thanks!"

4. **Personal stories outperform corporate content** -- "I" posts outperform "We" posts by 2-3x. People follow people, not brands.

5. **Carousels and documents get highest reach** -- the algorithm favors content that keeps people on platform. Carousels have the highest time-on-content.

6. **Write for one person, not your whole network** -- "this is for engineering managers who just started managing managers" is more compelling than "leadership tips for everyone."

## Common mistakes

1. **Starting with "I'm excited to announce"** -- this is the fastest way to get scrolled past. Start with value or surprise, not enthusiasm.

2. **Too many hashtags** -- more than 5 hashtags looks spammy and does not improve distribution. 3-5 relevant hashtags is optimal.

3. **Posting links in the main post** -- LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links. Put the link in the first comment instead, or use the featured section.

4. **Corporate voice on a personal profile** -- LinkedIn is a professional network of individuals. Write like a knowledgeable person, not like a press release.

5. **No engagement after posting** -- posting and disappearing kills distribution. Spend 15 minutes replying to comments and commenting on others' posts.
