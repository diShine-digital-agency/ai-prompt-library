# Social Media Content Calendar

Generate structured social media content calendars with platform-specific
posts, hashtag strategies, and engagement planning.

## When to Use

- Monthly or quarterly social media planning
- Multi-platform content coordination
- Brand awareness campaign scheduling
- Product launch social media rollout
- Consistent posting cadence establishment

## The Technique

A good social media calendar balances content pillars, platform-specific
formats, and strategic timing to maximize engagement.

## Template

```
Create a {{duration}}-week social media content calendar.

Brand: {{brand}}
Platforms: {{platforms}} (LinkedIn / Instagram / X / TikTok / Facebook)
Content themes: {{themes}} (e.g., thought leadership, product, culture, education)
Posting frequency: {{frequency}} per platform per week
Brand voice: {{voice_description}}

For each post, include:

| Day | Platform | Content Type | Post Copy | Hashtags | Visual Direction | CTA | Notes |
|-----|----------|-------------|-----------|----------|-----------------|-----|-------|

CONTENT PILLARS (distribute across the calendar):
- Educational ({{percentage}}%): Tips, how-tos, industry insights
- Promotional ({{percentage}}%): Product features, offers, launches
- Engagement ({{percentage}}%): Polls, questions, user-generated content
- Brand culture ({{percentage}}%): Behind-the-scenes, team, values

PLATFORM-SPECIFIC GUIDELINES:

LinkedIn:
- Professional tone, industry insights, thought leadership
- Optimal length: 150-300 words for text posts
- Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM
- Use carousel documents for frameworks and listicles
- Tag relevant people and companies

Instagram:
- Visual-first, lifestyle-oriented
- Captions: Hook in first line, value in body, CTA at end
- Mix: Feed posts, Stories, Reels
- Use 20-30 hashtags (mix of broad and niche)
- Best times: Tuesday-Friday, 11 AM-1 PM

X (Twitter):
- Concise, conversational, timely
- Thread format for longer insights
- Engage with industry conversations
- Best times: Monday-Friday, 8-10 AM
- Use 1-3 relevant hashtags

TikTok:
- Authentic, trend-aware, hook in first 3 seconds
- Educational content performs well for B2B
- Use trending sounds when relevant
- Post 1-3 times daily for best reach
- Vertical video format only
```

## Examples

### B2B SaaS Calendar (2-Week Sample)

```
Brand: CloudMetrics (analytics platform)
Platforms: LinkedIn, X
Themes: Data literacy, product updates, industry trends

Week 1:
Mon | LinkedIn | Carousel | "5 Dashboard Mistakes That Hide Your Best Insights" | #DataAnalytics #Dashboard | 5-slide carousel | "Download our template" | Educational
Tue | X | Thread | "I reviewed 100 dashboards this quarter. Here are the 3 patterns that separate good from great:" | #Analytics | None needed | "Reply with yours" | Engagement
Wed | LinkedIn | Text post | Share industry report with commentary | #MarTech #DataDriven | Report screenshot | "Read the full report" | Thought leadership
Thu | X | Poll | "What's your biggest analytics challenge?" with 4 options | #DataOps | Poll graphic | Vote + comment | Engagement
Fri | LinkedIn | Video | 60-sec product tip: "How to set up automated alerts" | #ProductTip | Screen recording | "Try it free" | Product
```

## Tips

1. **Batch content creation** — Plan a month ahead and create content in
   batches. This ensures consistency and reduces daily pressure.

2. **Repurpose across platforms** — A LinkedIn carousel can become an X thread,
   an Instagram carousel, and a short-form video. Plan repurposing from the start.

3. **Leave room for reactive content** — Reserve 20% of your calendar for
   trending topics, industry news, and timely engagement.

4. **Track and adjust** — Review engagement metrics weekly. Double down on
   what works, retire what does not.

5. **Use a content mix formula** — The 70-20-10 rule works well: 70% proven
   content types, 20% new formats, 10% experimental.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Same content everywhere** — Posting identical copy across LinkedIn, Instagram,
   and X ignores platform culture and format differences.

2. **All promotional** — Feeds that only promote products lose followers. Maintain
   the education/engagement balance.

3. **No visual direction** — "Post about our product" without visual guidance
   produces inconsistent brand presentation.

4. **Ignoring analytics** — A calendar without performance review cycles
   repeats underperforming content indefinitely.

5. **Over-scheduling** — Posting 3x daily on every platform is unsustainable
   and often reduces per-post engagement. Quality beats quantity.
