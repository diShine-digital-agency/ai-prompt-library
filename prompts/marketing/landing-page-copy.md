# Landing Page Copy Generator

Write high-converting landing page copy with compelling headlines, benefit-driven
sections, social proof placement, and clear calls to action.

## When to Use

- Launching a new product or feature
- A/B testing headline and CTA variations
- Rewriting underperforming landing pages
- Creating industry-specific landing page variants
- Building landing pages for ad campaigns with message match

## The Technique

Great landing page copy follows a proven structure: hook with a headline that
addresses the visitor's core pain, amplify the problem, present the solution,
prove it works, and close with a clear CTA. Every sentence earns the next
sentence. Specificity converts; vagueness bounces.

## Template

```
Write landing page copy for the following product.

## Product
Product name: {{product_name}}
What it does: {{value_proposition}}

## Audience
Target audience: {{target_audience}}
Their biggest pain points: {{pain_points}}

## Goal
Primary CTA goal: {{cta_goal}} (e.g., sign up for free trial, book a demo,
buy now, join waitlist)

## Structure
Generate the following sections:

### 1. Hero Section
- Headline (max 10 words): Address the #1 pain point with a clear benefit.
- Subheadline (max 25 words): Expand on how the product solves the problem.
- CTA button text (max 5 words)
- Supporting micro-copy below the CTA (max 15 words)

### 2. Problem Section
- Section headline
- 3 specific pain points the audience recognizes immediately
- Each pain point: 1-2 sentences, use the audience's own language

### 3. Solution Section
- Section headline
- 3 key features mapped directly to the pain points above
- Each feature: benefit-first description (what they get, not what it does)

### 4. Social Proof Section
- Section headline
- Placeholder for 2 customer testimonials with guidance on what to include
- Metrics line (e.g., "Trusted by X companies" or "Y% improvement")

### 5. Final CTA Section
- Urgency or value-reinforcement headline
- CTA button text (same or variant of hero CTA)
- Risk-reversal statement (e.g., "30-day money-back guarantee")

## Tone
- Conversational but credible
- Specific numbers over vague claims
- Active voice, short sentences
- No jargon unless the audience uses it daily
```

## Examples

### B2B SaaS Product

```
HERO:
Headline: Stop Losing Deals to Slow Proposals
Subheadline: Create polished proposals in 10 minutes, not 10 hours.
             Auto-pull pricing, case studies, and terms from your CRM.
CTA: Start Free Trial
Micro-copy: No credit card required. Set up in 2 minutes.

PROBLEM:
Your proposal process is costing you revenue.
- You spend 4+ hours copying data between tools for every proposal.
- Prospects go cold while your team manually formats documents.
- Pricing errors slip through because spreadsheets are not version-controlled.

SOLUTION:
- One-click proposals: Pull deal data directly from Salesforce. Zero re-typing.
- Real-time collaboration: Your team edits simultaneously. No more email chains.
- Error-proof pricing: Approved pricing tables lock automatically. No overrides.

CTA:
Headline: Every hour spent on proposals is an hour not spent selling.
CTA: Start Free Trial
Risk reversal: 30-day money-back guarantee. Cancel anytime.
```

## Tips

1. **Lead with outcomes, not features** — "Save 4 hours per proposal" beats
   "AI-powered proposal automation" every time.

2. **Mirror the audience's language** — Read reviews, support tickets, and
   forum posts from your target audience. Use their exact phrases.

3. **One CTA, one goal** — Every section should drive toward the same action.
   Multiple competing CTAs split attention and reduce conversion.

4. **Specificity builds trust** — "Trusted by 2,847 companies" is more credible
   than "Trusted by thousands of companies."

5. **Test headlines first** — The headline determines whether visitors read
   further. Generate 5-10 variants and A/B test before optimizing body copy.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Feature dumping** — Listing every feature overwhelms visitors. Pick the
   three that matter most to your audience and go deep on those.

2. **No clear CTA** — If visitors finish reading and do not know what to do
   next, the page has failed. Make the action obvious and repeat it.

3. **Ignoring mobile** — Over half of landing page traffic is mobile. Long
   paragraphs and tiny CTA buttons kill mobile conversion rates.

4. **Generic social proof** — "Great product!" from "Happy Customer" is
   worthless. Use real names, titles, companies, and specific results.

5. **Weak risk reversal** — "Try it today" without addressing risk (cost,
   time, commitment) leaves objections unresolved. Add guarantees.
