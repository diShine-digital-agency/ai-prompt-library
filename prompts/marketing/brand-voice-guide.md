# Brand Voice Guide Generator

Create comprehensive brand voice guidelines that ensure consistent communication
across all channels, teams, and content types.

## When to Use

- Establishing brand voice for a new company or product
- Documenting existing brand voice for team alignment
- Onboarding new writers, marketers, or agencies
- Rebranding or voice evolution exercises
- Multi-market brand localization

## The Technique

A brand voice guide defines not just how you sound, but why you sound that way
and how to adapt the voice across different contexts while maintaining consistency.

## Template

```
Create a comprehensive brand voice guide for:

Brand: {{brand}}
Core values: {{values}}
Target audience: {{audience}}
Industry: {{industry}}

Generate the following sections:

1. BRAND PERSONALITY
Define the brand as if it were a person:
- Age range and demographic feel
- Personality traits (5 adjectives with definitions)
- Communication style in one sentence
- If this brand were at a dinner party, how would it behave?

2. VOICE ATTRIBUTES
Define 4 core voice attributes as spectrums:

| Attribute | We are... | We are NOT... |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| {{attr_1}} | [description] | [opposite] |
| {{attr_2}} | [description] | [opposite] |
| {{attr_3}} | [description] | [opposite] |
| {{attr_4}} | [description] | [opposite] |

3. TONE VARIATIONS BY CONTEXT
Show how the same voice adapts:

| Context | Tone shift | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Website homepage | Confident, inviting | [example sentence] |
| Error message | Helpful, calm | [example sentence] |
| Social media | Casual, engaging | [example sentence] |
| Customer complaint | Empathetic, solution-focused | [example sentence] |
| Press release | Professional, authoritative | [example sentence] |
| Internal comms | Friendly, direct | [example sentence] |

4. LANGUAGE DO'S AND DON'TS

DO:
- [specific language pattern to use]
- [preferred vocabulary]
- [sentence structure preferences]

DON'T:
- [language to avoid]
- [words on the banned list]
- [patterns that conflict with voice]

5. GRAMMAR AND STYLE CHOICES
- Oxford comma: yes/no
- Contractions: always/sometimes/never
- Exclamation marks: frequency guidance
- Emoji usage: when and where
- Capitalization: title case vs. sentence case
- Numbers: spell out or use digits

6. SAMPLE CONTENT
Rewrite these standard messages in your brand voice:
- Welcome email
- 404 error page
- Product update announcement
- Customer thank-you note
- Apology for a service disruption
```

## Examples

### Tech Startup Voice Guide

```
Brand: Nimble (project management tool)
Values: Simplicity, empowerment, transparency
Audience: Creative teams and small agencies

BRAND PERSONALITY:
Nimble is like a smart, organized friend who makes everything
feel manageable. Late 20s energy — modern and capable but never
condescending. At a dinner party, Nimble would be the one who
quietly organized the seating chart so everyone sits next to
someone interesting.

VOICE ATTRIBUTES:
| Clear, not clever | We explain simply. No puns or wordplay that obscures meaning. |
| Warm, not bubbly | We're friendly and human. Not over-caffeinated or performative. |
| Confident, not arrogant | We know our product is good. We don't need to shout about it. |
| Helpful, not hovering | We offer guidance when asked. We don't over-explain or patronize. |

TONE EXAMPLES:
Website: "Manage projects without the overhead. Nimble keeps your team moving."
Error: "That page took a wrong turn. Let's get you back on track."
Social: "Hot take: your to-do list shouldn't need its own to-do list."
Complaint: "That's not the experience you should have. Here's what we're doing about it."
```

## Tips

1. **Use real examples** — Abstract voice descriptions ("we're friendly") mean
   different things to different writers. Show, don't just tell.

2. **Include anti-examples** — "We are NOT..." is often clearer than "We are..."
   Showing what you are not defines boundaries effectively.

3. **Create a word bank** — Maintain lists of preferred words and banned words.
   "Challenge" instead of "problem," "explore" instead of "check out."

4. **Test with multiple writers** — Give the voice guide to 3 writers and have
   them each write the same message. If the outputs are consistent, the guide works.

5. **Version the guide** — Brand voice evolves. Date the guide and plan
   annual reviews to keep it aligned with brand evolution.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too vague** — "We're professional yet approachable" describes every brand.
   Get specific about what professional-yet-approachable means for your brand.

2. **No context variation** — A brand that sounds identical in a tweet and a
   legal notice feels robotic. Show how tone adapts while voice stays constant.

3. **Aspirational vs. actual** — Writing a voice guide for who you want to be,
   not who you are, creates disconnect. Start from your current authentic voice.

4. **Missing the don'ts** — Writers need to know what to avoid as much as what
   to do. Banned words and anti-patterns prevent off-brand content.

5. **No sample content** — A voice guide without example copy is a theory
   document. Include 5-10 rewritten samples across different content types.
