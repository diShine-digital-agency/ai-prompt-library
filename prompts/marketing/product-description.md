# Product Description Generator

Write compelling, SEO-friendly product descriptions that highlight benefits,
address buyer objections, and drive purchases on e-commerce platforms.

## When to Use

- Listing new products on e-commerce platforms
- Rewriting underperforming product descriptions
- Adapting descriptions for different marketplaces (Amazon, Shopify, Etsy)
- Scaling product catalog copy for hundreds of SKUs
- A/B testing description variants for conversion optimization

## The Technique

Great product descriptions sell outcomes, not specifications. Lead with the
benefit the buyer cares about most, weave in sensory or emotional language,
and place technical specs where comparison shoppers expect them. Structure
the description to work for both skimmers (bullet points) and readers
(narrative paragraph).

## Template

```
Write a product description for the following item.

## Product Details
Product name: {{product_name}}
Category: {{category}}
Key features and specifications: {{features}}

## Buyer Profile
Target buyer: {{target_buyer}}
What problem does this product solve for them?
What objections might they have before purchasing?

## Tone
Write in a {{tone}} tone (e.g., professional, playful, luxurious,
minimalist, technical, warm).

## Platform
This description will appear on {{platform}} (e.g., Amazon, Shopify,
company website, Etsy, Walmart Marketplace).

## Structure
1. HEADLINE (max 80 characters)
   Include the primary keyword. Lead with the benefit or use case.

2. SHORT DESCRIPTION (2-3 sentences)
   Hook the buyer. Address their #1 concern or desire. This appears
   above the fold on most platforms.

3. BULLET POINTS (5 bullets)
   - Start each bullet with a bold benefit, followed by the feature.
   - Include dimensions, materials, or specs where relevant.
   - Address one common objection in at least one bullet.

4. LONG DESCRIPTION (100-150 words)
   Tell a mini story: who this product is for, how it fits into their
   life, and why this version is better than alternatives.

5. SEO KEYWORDS
   List 5 relevant long-tail keywords to incorporate naturally.

## Rules
- Never use superlatives without proof ("best" requires a citation).
- Avoid clichés: "game-changer", "revolutionary", "next-level".
- Include at least one specific number (measurement, capacity, weight).
- Write for scannability: short paragraphs, white space, bold key terms.
```

## Examples

### Wireless Noise-Cancelling Headphones

```
HEADLINE:
Studio-Quality Noise Cancelling Headphones — 40-Hour Battery Life

SHORT DESCRIPTION:
Block out the world and focus. The SoundPro NC-500 delivers studio-grade
active noise cancellation with 40 hours of battery life, so your music
outlasts every flight, commute, and work session.

BULLET POINTS:
- 40-Hour Battery Life: A full work week on a single charge. Quick-charge
  gives 3 hours of playback from a 10-minute charge.
- Adaptive Noise Cancellation: 3 ANC levels adjust to your environment —
  full isolation for flights, transparency mode for conversations.
- Premium Comfort: Memory foam ear cushions and 8.5 oz weight mean
  all-day wear without pressure or fatigue.
- Hi-Res Audio Certified: 40mm custom drivers deliver frequency response
  up to 40kHz for detail you can hear in every track.
- Built for Travel: Folds flat into the included hard case. FAA-approved
  Bluetooth for in-flight use.

LONG DESCRIPTION:
Designed for professionals who spend hours in noisy environments, the
SoundPro NC-500 turns any space into your personal studio. Three levels of
adaptive noise cancellation let you dial in the right amount of isolation,
whether you are on a cross-country flight or in an open office. The memory
foam cushions and balanced weight distribution were tested in 200+ hours
of continuous wear sessions. Pairs with two devices simultaneously, so
you can switch between your laptop and phone without reconnecting.

SEO KEYWORDS:
noise cancelling headphones long battery, wireless headphones for travel,
comfortable over-ear headphones, active noise cancellation bluetooth,
headphones for office work
```

## Tips

1. **Lead with the benefit, not the feature** — "40-hour battery life" is a
   feature. "A full work week on a single charge" is the benefit.

2. **Research the platform's guidelines** — Amazon has character limits and
   prohibits HTML in bullets. Shopify allows rich text. Match the format.

3. **Include dimensions and weight** — Online buyers cannot touch the product.
   Specific measurements reduce returns and increase confidence.

4. **Address the top objection** — Read 1-star reviews of competing products.
   Pre-answer the most common complaint in your description.

5. **Use natural keyword placement** — Stuffing keywords ruins readability
   and can hurt search rankings. Weave them into benefit-driven sentences.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Feature lists without benefits** — "Bluetooth 5.3" means nothing to
   most buyers. "Connects instantly, stays connected 30 feet away" does.

2. **Writing for yourself, not the buyer** — Engineers love specs. Buyers
   love outcomes. Balance both, but lead with the outcome.

3. **Identical descriptions across platforms** — Amazon shoppers skim bullets.
   Etsy shoppers read stories. Adapt the format to each marketplace.

4. **No social proof integration** — "Rated 4.8 stars by 2,000+ customers"
   in the description reinforces confidence. Omitting proof wastes trust.

5. **Ignoring mobile formatting** — Most e-commerce browsing is on mobile.
   Long paragraphs without line breaks are unreadable on small screens.
