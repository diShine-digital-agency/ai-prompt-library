# Pitch Deck Outline Generator

Create compelling pitch deck outlines with slide-by-slide content,
speaker notes, and data visualization recommendations.

## When to Use

- Fundraising presentations (seed, Series A, B, etc.)
- Corporate innovation pitches
- Partnership and business development presentations
- Internal project funding requests
- Accelerator and competition applications

## The Technique

A pitch deck tells a story: here is the problem, here is our solution,
here is why now, here is how we win, and here is what we need.

## Template

```
Create a pitch deck outline for:

Company: {{company_name}}
Stage: {{stage}} (pre-seed / seed / Series A / growth / corporate)
Ask: {{funding_amount_or_request}}
Audience: {{audience}} (VCs / angels / corporate / internal)
Industry: {{industry}}
Unique angle: {{what_makes_this_different}}
Time limit: {{minutes}} minutes

Generate slide-by-slide outline:

SLIDE 1: TITLE
- Company name and one-line description
- Presenter name and title
- Visual: Logo on clean background

SLIDE 2: PROBLEM
- The problem in one sentence
- Who experiences it and how often
- Current cost of the problem (quantified)
- Why existing solutions fall short
- Speaker notes: Personal story or customer quote

SLIDE 3: SOLUTION
- Your solution in one sentence
- How it works (3 bullet points max)
- Key differentiator (what makes this approach unique)
- Visual: Product screenshot or architecture diagram

SLIDE 4: DEMO / PRODUCT
- Live demo or product walkthrough
- Key user flow (3-5 steps)
- "Wow moment" — the feature that makes people lean in
- Visual: Product screenshots or demo video link

SLIDE 5: MARKET OPPORTUNITY
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): ${{tam}}
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): ${{sam}}
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): ${{som}}
- Market growth rate and trends
- Visual: Market size funnel or growth chart

SLIDE 6: BUSINESS MODEL
- How you make money (revenue streams)
- Pricing model and unit economics
- Average contract value or ARPU
- Expansion and upsell potential
- Visual: Pricing tiers or revenue flow diagram

SLIDE 7: TRACTION
- Key metrics that prove momentum
- Growth rate (month-over-month or quarter-over-quarter)
- Customer count, revenue, or usage milestones
- Notable logos or customer quotes
- Visual: Growth chart (hockey stick preferred)

SLIDE 8: COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
- 2x2 positioning matrix
- Key differentiators vs. competitors
- Sustainable competitive advantages (moat)
- Why now — what changed to make this possible
- Visual: Positioning map or comparison table

SLIDE 9: GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY
- Customer acquisition channels
- Sales motion (self-serve / inside / enterprise)
- Partnership strategy
- Customer acquisition cost and payback period
- Visual: Funnel with conversion rates

SLIDE 10: TEAM
- Founders with relevant experience (one line each)
- Key hires and advisors
- Why this team is uniquely positioned to win
- Open roles you are hiring for
- Visual: Team photos with credentials

SLIDE 11: FINANCIALS
- Revenue projections (3-year)
- Key assumptions behind projections
- Path to profitability
- Burn rate and runway
- Visual: Revenue projection chart with milestones

SLIDE 12: THE ASK
- Funding amount: ${{amount}}
- Use of funds breakdown (hiring, product, marketing, etc.)
- Milestones this funding will achieve
- Target timeline for next round
- Visual: Use of funds pie chart and milestone timeline

SLIDE 13: CLOSING
- One compelling sentence that summarizes the opportunity
- Contact information
- Appendix slides available (detailed financials, technical architecture)
```

## Examples

### SaaS Series A Pitch

```
Company: DataLens — Real-time analytics for e-commerce
Stage: Series A
Ask: $8M
Traction: $1.2M ARR, 85 customers, 15% MoM growth

SLIDE 7 — TRACTION:
"From 0 to $1.2M ARR in 14 months"
- 85 paying customers (avg contract: $14K/year)
- 15% month-over-month revenue growth
- 120% net dollar retention
- Notable customers: [3 recognizable logos]
- NPS: 72 (industry average: 31)
Visual: Revenue chart from $0 to $1.2M with monthly data points

SLIDE 12 — THE ASK:
"$8M Series A to reach $5M ARR in 18 months"
Use of funds:
- Engineering (45%): Hire 8 engineers, build enterprise features
- Sales (30%): Build outbound sales team, 3 AEs + 1 SDR
- Marketing (15%): Content, events, demand generation
- Operations (10%): Infrastructure, compliance, admin
```

## Tips

1. **10-20-30 rule** — 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt minimum font. More slides
   dilute impact. Fewer minutes force clarity.

2. **Lead with the problem** — Investors fund solutions to painful problems.
   If the problem is not compelling, the solution does not matter.

3. **Show traction, not projections** — Real metrics beat forecasts. If you
   have customers, lead with that. Projections are secondary.

4. **One idea per slide** — Each slide should communicate one key message.
   If you need two points, use two slides.

5. **Practice the 2-minute version** — Be able to pitch the entire deck in
   2 minutes for elevator pitch scenarios and time-crunched meetings.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too many slides** — A 30-slide pitch deck signals inability to prioritize.
   Keep the core deck under 15 slides; details go in the appendix.

2. **No traction slide** — Even pre-revenue startups have traction: waitlist
   signups, LOIs, pilot results, engagement metrics.

3. **Unrealistic projections** — "$1B revenue in 5 years" without credible
   assumptions undermines credibility. Ground projections in bottoms-up math.

4. **Underselling the team** — Investors bet on teams, especially at early
   stages. Highlight domain expertise and relevant experience.

5. **Vague use of funds** — "We'll use the money to grow" is not a plan.
   Specific headcount, channel spending, and milestone targets show maturity.
